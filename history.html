Bagels in New York

Bagels were brought to New York by Jewish immigrants from Eastern Europe. Over time, they became a major part of the city’s food identity. New York bagels became famous for their chewy inside and shiny crust, which come from boiling the dough before baking it. Today, bagels are sold in delis, bakeries, and bagel shops all over the city, and they remain one of the most classic New York breakfast foods.
